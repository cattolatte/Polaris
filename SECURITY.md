# Security Policy

## 🛡️ Reporting a Security Vulnerability

The Polaris team and community take security issues seriously. We appreciate your efforts to responsibly disclose your findings, and we will make every effort to acknowledge your contributions.

To report a security vulnerability, please use the **GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting** feature.

> **Please do not report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues, discussions, or other public channels.**

When reporting, please provide a clear and concise description, including:

- A summary of the vulnerability.
- The potential impact.
- Detailed steps to reproduce the issue.
- Any suggested mitigation or fix, if you have one.

---

## Responsible Disclosure

Our team will acknowledge your report within 48 hours and will work with you to understand and validate the issue. We will provide regular updates on our progress.

We follow a policy of responsible disclosure:

1.  Once a report is received, we will confirm the vulnerability and determine its severity.
2.  A fix will be developed and tested.
3.  We will coordinate with you on a public disclosure date. We aim to release a patch before or at the same time as the public disclosure.

---

## Supported Versions

Polaris is currently in its early, pre-release development phase (`v0.x.y`). As such, security patches will only be provided for the **latest development version** on the main branch.

| Version | Supported          |
| :------ | :----------------- |
| `0.x.y` | :white_check_mark: |
| `< 0.x` | :x:                |

---

Thank you for helping keep Polaris secure.
